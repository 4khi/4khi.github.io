Before we begin in our journey of taking precautions against the kuffar online we must make dua to Allah SubhanahuWaTa'la to beg him for his help in this matter.

 

It is crucial that we as muslims take our precations against the kuffar. They are tracking us and we should not allow them to gain an upper hand against us. The consequences of us not taking the correct measures are devastating. For example, if the khilafah is established and the administration are using Windows computers which are closed source, then the khilafah will be at  high risk of political espionage as one cannot know what is running as the Windows source code has not been released and as such we should assume there is a hidden backdoor.

Operating System:

Do not use any sort of closed source (software for which code has not been released) OS such as Windows or MacOS as they WILL have backdoors for the the tawaghit governments to track and monitor everything you do on your machine. The internet mujahid should only use a completely open source Linux operating system such as:

  1. PureOS (easiest to use)
  2. Trisquel (easy to use)
  3. Parabola (hard to configure)
  4. Hyperbola (hard to configure)

 

The beauty of  open source software, is that one can verify that there are no backdoors or other malicious, hidden software. One can have privacy by verifiable design, not by trust.

Tor browser: A powerful anonymisation tool.

Tor works on a trustless design whereby you do not need to trust those running the tor servers and nodes

Methods to hide the fact you use TOR from your ISP and therefore the tawaghit government  as the ISP will give logs to the tawaghit when asked to:

1) VPN.

One should only use a VPN to hide the fact you use tor. Do not use a VPN by itself as it requires that you trust the VPN provider unlike tor where you do not need to trust any of the tor node , relay or bridge providers.  VPN servers are also exteremely attractive targets for hackers. A good free VPN is ProtonVPN

2) Bridges

A bridge is simply an unlisted Tor guard relay. Your ISP (or VPN provider) will not know that you are connecting to the tor network as the tor node is not listed as such.

3) Bridge with a pluggable transport. Pluggable transports make Tor traffic look different so it can (InShaAllah) not be fingerprinted and then blocked. obfs4 is a popular PT that makes the Tor traffic between you and your bridge look like encrypted garbage. meek is another PT that makes it look like you are  talking to a website in the Amazon or Microsoft cloud. To use a bridge with pluggable transport click configure instead of connect when you start up tor and select the bridge you wish to use and then connect to tor

It is best practise to use a VPN and  bridge with a pluggable transport to hide tor usage. This can be slow. To mitigate  one can use ethernet instead of WiFi or only use a tor bridge.
